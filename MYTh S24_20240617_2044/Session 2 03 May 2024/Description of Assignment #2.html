Prepare Your Preliminary Proposal!


Start by framing your thesis proposal with a compelling introduction to the research you are contemplating for your thesis. If you have not yet decided on a topic for your thesis, please choose a topic that might be a potential candidate for you to write on. Your answers to the below questions may help you develop a more accurate picture of the work you intend to execute.

Later, you may incorporate the answers to these inquiries into your final thesis proposal. When preparing the final thesis proposal, please consider the template I have shown in our session and the Assessment Sheet that could be looked upon as a list of requirements.

As a final MasterÂ´s Thesis Proposal should be at most six pages long, keep your answers in this preliminary proposal short to fit three to four pages (additional to the title page). Use 1.5 line spacing for better readability and follow the file naming pattern YourName_Pre-proposal_S24.

These are the questions to lead you:

1. What problem are you addressing?
2. What is the contribution to your field of research/inquiry?
3. Why is this problem important? (Why would this work be meaningful to someone not committed to your field?)
4. How are you going to attack the problem? (How will you subdivide the main task into manageable chunks?)
5. What resources will you need to reach your goal?

NB:
You are not required to hand in your outline again or write it anew. However, please include your preliminary schedule in your pre-proposal.
Although it may be reasonable to prepare this preliminary proposal on the same topic your outline was about, you are not obliged to do so.
